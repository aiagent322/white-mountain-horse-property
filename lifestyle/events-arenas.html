Linden Valley Arena — Events, Facilities & Calendar

Linden Valley Arena is the equestrian anchor of the White Mountains. Located in Linden, Arizona off Pearce Road at Mile Marker 335 on Highway 260, the arena operates as a premier team roping destination and the community hub for the entire White Mountains horse world. If you own horse property near Linden, your social calendar runs through this arena from May through October.

The Facilities

Linden Valley Arena operates two full-size roping arenas and a warm-up arena — large enough to host two separate events simultaneously. Facilities include:

  • Stalls: 205 uncovered stalls ($23/night, $150/month) and 12 covered stalls ($52/night, $200/month)
  • RV Hookups: 18 full hookup spots with 35- and 50-amp power, water, sewer, and trash dumpster access ($50/night, $800/month)
  • Cook Shack: On-site food and refreshments
  • Clean restroom facilities
  • Playground
  • Free parking

Stall and RV reservations are made online at lindenvalleyarena.com. The arena also has on-site advertising available for local businesses and runs a shop and a referral parking reservation system.

World Series of Team Roping (WSTR)

Linden Valley Arena (formally "Rock-N-Roll LLC, DBA Linden Valley Arena") is an official WSTR qualifier venue. The WSTR is the national amateur handicapped team roping series, and Linden qualifiers draw competitors from across Arizona and neighboring states. The arena hosts multiple WSTR events throughout the season — confirmed in the WSTR national calendar for 2026. Participants can register through GlobalHandicaps.com.

Beast Truck Team Roping

The Beast Truck Team Roping is a major sponsored roping event held twice at Linden Valley Arena in 2026 (June and August). These two-day events draw significant participation and are among the higher-profile events on the annual calendar beyond the WSTR qualifiers.

Show Low Rodeo

The annual Show Low Rodeo is held at Linden Valley Arena — hosted by the City of Show Low. The 2026 rodeo runs June 5–6, with Friday as a youth rodeo (ages 18 and under) and Saturday as an open rodeo at 6 p.m. Events include breakaway roping, parent/offspring roping, barrel racing, ribbon roping, hangman's race, mutton busting, calf riding, steer riding, junior bull riding, ranch bronc, saddle bronc, and bareback. This is the signature western community event of the White Mountains summer season.

Directions

From Highway 260, turn north on Pearce Road at Perkins Cinders — approximately Mile Marker 335. Free on-site parking. Follow Linden Valley Arena's website and social media for event-day parking details.
