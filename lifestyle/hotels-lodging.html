White Mountains Hotels & Lodging Directory

Whether you're scouting a horse property in Linden, competing at a WSTR qualifier at Linden Valley Arena, or riding the White Mountain Trail System for a week — you need somewhere to stay. This directory covers the best-rated hotels across the region, organized by community, with honest notes drawn from verified guest reviews. Properties are rated on a 5-star scale from Google. Show Low has the largest and most reliable hotel inventory; Pinetop-Lakeside has more cabin and resort options; and the eastern communities of Springerville and Eagar have good-value properties for riders accessing the Round Valley Rodeo and the Apache-Sitgreaves backcountry.

Show Low — Best Hotel Selection in the Region

Show Low's position as the White Mountains' regional service hub extends to lodging. The Deuce of Clubs corridor (Highway 60/Show Low's main street) has the highest concentration of national chain hotels in the region, and two newer properties near Woolford Road represent the top tier. Show Low hotels are the right base for anyone competing at or attending Linden Valley Arena events — most properties are within 10–15 minutes of the arena.

Hampton Inn & Suites Show Low–Pinetop

1501 E Woolford Rd, Show Low  ·  (928) 532-4444
★ 4.6
903 reviews

The top-rated hotel in Show Low proper. A newer property on Woolford Road — guests consistently praise cleanliness, well-maintained facilities, and standout breakfast. A reviewer noted seeing a herd of elk from their room window and horses nearby, which tells you something about the setting. Pool, fitness center, free hot breakfast. Consistently 16+ years old but kept in excellent condition. Best choice for Linden Valley Arena event weekends.

Fairfield by Marriott Inn & Suites Show Low

1401 E Woolford Rd, Show Low  ·  (928) 320-5002
★ 4.6
72 reviews

Adjacent to the Hampton on Woolford Road — newer Marriott property with heated pool, free breakfast, and friendly staff. Fewer reviews than the Hampton but equally strong ratings. Guests highlight the clean modern rooms and kind service touches — one reviewer received a birthday snack package that had been noted at booking. Good pick if Hampton is sold out during major Arena weekends.

Holiday Inn Express Show Low

151 W Deuce of Clubs, Show Low  ·  (928) 537-5115
★ 4.3
566 reviews

Solid mid-range option on the Deuce of Clubs corridor — pool, fitness center, free breakfast. Centrally located to Show Low's services and walkable to several restaurants. Reviews praise the breakfast quality and fresh coffee. Pool has had occasional maintenance closures — confirm availability if that matters for your stay.

Best Western Paint Pony Lodge

581 W Deuce of Clubs, Show Low  ·  (928) 537-5773
★ 4.4
532 reviews

A consistently well-rated mid-range pick on the western Deuce of Clubs end. Guests praise immaculate rooms, genuinely attentive service, comfortable beds, and a good continental breakfast with fresh fruit. No pool, but staff provide passes to the nearby city aquatic center. The name is appropriately western. Good value choice for the area.

Days Inn by Wyndham Show Low

480 W Deuce of Clubs, Show Low  ·  (928) 892-5029
★ 4.1
1,094 reviews

Most-reviewed hotel in Show Low — the volume speaks to its position as an established go-to on the corridor. On-site Show Low Café (separate business, highly regarded) is a draw. A-frame building units, hot breakfast included, fridge and microwave in rooms. Buildings show age and the pool area has had complaints — but guests rate the friendliness and location highly. A solid backup when higher-rated properties are full during Arena weekends.

Pinetop-Lakeside — Cabins, Resorts & Traditional Hotels

Pinetop-Lakeside has a different lodging character than Show Low — more cabins, resorts, and lake-adjacent properties, fewer national chains. For equestrian buyers, remember that most Pinetop-Lakeside subdivisions prohibit horses on private property (see the Pinetop-Lakeside warning page) — but it remains a popular base for trail riding access, Linden Valley Arena events, and the broader White Mountains lifestyle.

Lake of the Woods Resort

2244 W White Mountain Blvd, Lakeside  ·  (928) 368-5353
★ 4.5
549 reviews

The best-reviewed traditional lodging in the Lakeside area — a family-owned resort with lakeside cabin units set back in the pines. Guests who have returned for 20 years cite the ownership and the mountain atmosphere as reasons they keep coming back. The setting is genuine: small lake, ponderosa pines, the kind of cool-and-quiet mountain pace the region is known for. Some units face Highway 260 with road noise; request a back unit. Best choice for a lakeside mountain experience in the Pinetop-Lakeside area.

PVC at The Roundhouse Resort

5801 Buck Springs Rd, Pinetop  ·  (928) 369-5100
★ 4.7
361 reviews

Highest-rated lodging in the Pinetop-Lakeside area. Cabin-style units that are described as surprisingly spacious, clean, and genuinely comfortable — more like a private cabin than a hotel room. Amenities include mini-golf and outdoor areas; some amenities operate on weekend-only schedules. Off the main corridor on Buck Springs Road — quieter and more private than the highway-facing properties. Good for a multi-night stay while scouting horse properties in the area.

Hon-Dah Resort Hotel

777 AZ-260, Pinetop  ·  (928) 369-0299
★ 4.2
650 reviews

White Mountain Apache Tribe-owned resort and casino on Highway 260 at the Fort Apache Reservation boundary — a distinctive property with a beautiful interior lobby. The hotel component is well-regarded (comfortable rooms, good food service); the casino reviews are mixed. For equestrian visitors, this is the closest hotel to Sunrise Park Resort and the tribal recreation areas. The on-site restaurant is worth knowing about. Note: tribal permit sold on-site — convenient if you're planning tribal land recreation.

Best Western Inn of Pinetop

404 E White Mountain Blvd, Pinetop-Lakeside  ·  (928) 367-6667
★ 4.1
495 reviews

Mid-range property on the White Mountain Boulevard corridor — reliable Best Western quality. Guests cite clean rooms, a good breakfast, and friendly staff. Older property; some rooms have noise issues from adjacent rooms. Charming restaurant next door with a well-regarded breakfast menu. Reasonable rates compared to the resort properties. Good mid-range pick for the Pinetop-Lakeside corridor.

GreenTree Inn & Suites Pinetop

431 E White Mountain Blvd, Pinetop  ·  (928) 367-6077
★ 4.0
489 reviews

Notable for its indoor heated pool open until 11 p.m. — rare in the region and popular with families. Elevator, bar, western décor including an elk head. Older property showing some deferred maintenance, but well-priced and in a good location on White Mountain Boulevard. Good breakfast. A solid budget pick, especially if the indoor pool matters.

Heber-Overgaard — Mogollon Rim Gateway

Heber-Overgaard is 38 miles west of Show Low on Highway 260 — the gateway from the Phoenix/Payson direction. Three solid lodging options here for riders accessing the Rim Lakes Recreation Area, the Mogollon Rim trail network, or just stopping en route to Show Low and Linden Valley Arena.

WorldMark Bison Ranch

2361 Bison Ranch Trl, Overgaard  ·  (928) 535-3010
★ 4.5
618 reviews

Best-rated lodging in Heber-Overgaard — a Wyndham timeshare resort with nightly and weekly rentals. Condo-style units with full kitchens, fireplaces, private balconies, hot tub, pool, and western décor. The 2-bedroom units are spacious and well-maintained. A restaurant, coffee shop, taproom, and store are on-site (some weekend-only hours). Genuinely one of the better-valued extended-stay options in the region for horse property scouting trips. Best pick for Heber-Overgaard area stays, especially multi-night.

Rusty Buck Inn

1842 AZ-260, Heber  ·  (928) 535-4598
★ 4.4
158 reviews

A well-named, no-fuss highway property on AZ-260 in Heber with keypad entry, clean rooms, comfortable beds, and good water pressure. Continental breakfast included. Contactless check-in via door code — no front desk. Some highway noise from the 260 corridor. One of the better budget options in the western White Mountains approach. Confirm pet policy and room type when booking — a subset of rooms are pet-friendly.

Big Pine Cabins

2268 AZ-260, Overgaard  ·  (928) 535-5649
★ 4.2
37 reviews

Small pet-friendly cabin property on Highway 260 in Overgaard — no pet fees noted in reviews, which is a genuine differentiator. Cozy individual cabins with hot tubs, full kitchens, and western cabin décor. Guests describe them as "adorable" and "like staying in your own getaway cabin." Short highway noise during the day, quiets by 9 p.m. Walking distance to a store and restaurant. Small property — book early for summer weekends.

Best Western Sawmill Inn

1877 AZ-260, Heber-Overgaard  ·  (928) 535-5053
★ 4.0
552 reviews

The largest traditional hotel in Heber-Overgaard — long-term stay guests rate the staff and quietness highly. Standard Best Western quality. Reviews have noted pricing discrepancies between online listings and walk-in rates; confirm your rate at booking. EV charging available on-site. Good option when WorldMark is unavailable.

Springerville & Eagar — Eastern White Mountains

Springerville and Eagar anchor the eastern White Mountains — the gateway to the Round Valley Rodeo (held every July 4th since 1912), the Apache-Sitgreaves backcountry, Escudilla Wilderness, and the Big Lake / Crescent Lake trout fishery. Lodging here is primarily independent and good-value. Reed's Lodge is the standout property in the entire eastern corridor.

Reed's Lodge ⭐ Regional Standout

514 E Main St, Springerville  ·  (928) 333-4323
★ 4.6
871 reviews

The highest-rated and most-reviewed lodging in the eastern White Mountains — a 1949 motor lodge renovated with care and character. Individual rooms are themed (Dr. Who's Spaceship is a real option) and guests consistently praise the cleanliness, comfortable beds, walk-in showers, retro-cool atmosphere, and welcoming staff. Continental breakfast included. The best lodging value in the Springerville/Eagar area — book early, especially for Round Valley Rodeo weekend (4th of July).

Best Western Sunrise Inn

128 N Main St, Eagar  ·  (928) 333-2540
★ 4.0
325 reviews

Standard Best Western on Eagar's Main Street — straightforward, reliable, and well-located for the 4th of July parade and rodeo (the parade passes on Main Street). Supermarket directly across the parking lot is convenient. Property is noted as overdue for remodeling; rooms are functional. Staff noted as genuinely kind, including bereavement pricing accommodation cited by one reviewer. Good backup option for Round Valley Rodeo weekend when Reed's is sold out.

Howard Johnson by Wyndham Springerville

242 E Main St, Springerville  ·  (928) 333-4365
★ 3.9
329 reviews

Colorful older property on Main Street in Springerville — suites available, pet-friendly, good breakfast room with strong variety. Guests note good housekeeping and kind staff but inconsistent floor cleanliness. A reasonable budget choice for a solo rider or a short overnight stop. Confirm hot water availability — reviews have flagged this issue in some rooms.

Booking Notes for Horse Property Visitors

  • Linden Valley Arena event weekends fill fast. WSTR qualifiers, Beast Truck Team Roping, and the Show Low Rodeo draw competitors from across the state. Book Show Low hotels 4–8 weeks in advance for major arena weekends. The Hampton Inn and Fairfield on Woolford Road sell out first.
  • RV hookups at Linden Valley Arena are available at $50/night (18 spots, 35 and 50-amp, full hookups including sewer). If you're competing with horses, staying on-site with your rig may be more practical than hauling back to a Show Low hotel every night. Reserve at resnexus.com.
  • Cabins are the alternative to hotels. Pinetop-Lakeside in particular has a large inventory of vacation cabin rentals through VRBO and Airbnb — many in the pines, some with private outdoor areas suitable for a horse trailer or equipment storage. Search specifically for "horse-friendly" or "no HOA" if you need to bring a rig.
  • Summer weekends (June–August) book out region-wide. The Phoenix-area summer escape market means White Mountains lodging is in high demand May through October — the same season as Linden Valley Arena's full event calendar.
  • For property scouting trips, the Hampton Inn or Fairfield in Show Low provide the best base — central to Linden, Pinedale, and Show Low proper, close to the arena, and reliably clean and comfortable.
